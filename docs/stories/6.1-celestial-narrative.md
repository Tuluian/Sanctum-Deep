# Story 6.1 Narrative: The Celestial — Seraphina Lightborn

## Status: Approved

## Story

**As a** player choosing the Celestial class,
**I want** a rich narrative context that explains my divine powers and conflicted origin,
**so that** Radiance mechanics feel meaningful and my journey has emotional weight.

---

## Narrative Context

> *"I thought I was chosen. Blessed. Special. Turns out I was just... occupied. But you know what? The power is still real. And now it's mine."*

**True Name:** Seraphina (born Sara of Millhaven)

### Who She Was

Sara grew up ordinary. Daughter of a farmer and a seamstress in a village so small it didn't appear on most maps. She had no destiny. No calling. No special gift.

Until the angel appeared.

At seven years old, Sara woke to find a being of light standing at her bedside. It called itself **Auriel**, a messenger of the divine. It told her she was chosen—marked from birth to serve as a vessel for celestial power.

Auriel touched her forehead. Light poured in. Sara became *radiant*.

### The Living Saint

Sara became Seraphina. Her village built a shrine to her. Pilgrims came from across the kingdom, seeking miracles. She healed the sick with golden light. She blessed crops with her touch. She spoke words of comfort that seemed to come from somewhere beyond.

For twelve years, Seraphina was worshipped. Adored. Revered.

And she hated every moment.

Because something was *wrong*. Auriel never spoke to her again after that first night. The light she channeled felt hungry, not holy. And sometimes, in the quiet hours, she heard whispers beneath the radiance—whispers that sounded like *screaming*.

### The Truth

At nineteen, Seraphina demanded answers. She fasted for forty days. She prayed until her voice broke. She begged Auriel to explain what she really was.

Auriel finally appeared.

It laughed.

*"You thought you were chosen? Sweet child. You were convenient. An empty vessel. A battery. I've been feeding on your worshippers' faith for twelve years, and you—you powered the consumption."*

Auriel wasn't an angel. It was a *fragment*—a piece of a dead god, consumed by the Hollow God millennia ago, surviving only as a parasitic shard of power. It had latched onto Sara because she was spiritually "empty"—no strong identity, no firm beliefs, nothing to resist the intrusion.

Seraphina wasn't blessed. She was *infested*.

### Why She Enters

When Auriel revealed the truth, it also revealed its fear: the Sanctum. The place where gods go to die. The fragment's "parent" was consumed there, and Auriel dreads being drawn back.

But Seraphina realizes: if Auriel's source is in the Sanctum, maybe she can find a way to purge it there. Maybe she can confront whatever remains of the consumed god and demand her body back.

Or maybe she'll learn to *keep* the power, but on her own terms.

She enters the Sanctum not as a vessel, but as a reckoning.

## Character Arc

### Act 1: Borrowed Power

Seraphina's radiance works magnificently in the Entrance Halls. The undead recoil from her light. The Bonelord's pride cannot stand against her brilliance.

But Auriel's voice grows louder the deeper she goes. *"We shouldn't be here. Turn back. Please. They'll find me here."*

For the first time, Seraphina hears fear in Auriel's voice. Real fear. The thing that possessed her is terrified.

**Theme:** Power that isn't truly yours.

### Act 2: Understanding the Parasite

The Flooded Depths resonate with Auriel. The fragment recognizes this place—recognizes the *feeling* of being consumed.

*"It was quick,"* Auriel whispers. *"My god—our god—the God of Dawn. The Hollow God ate it in an instant. A thousand years of worship, gone. I only survived because I was the smallest piece. The forgotten fragment. The part even my god didn't remember."*

Seraphina realizes: Auriel isn't a monster. It's a *refugee*. A fragment of something that was murdered, clinging to existence through her. It parasitized her because it was desperate to exist. Because the alternative was the void.

**Theme:** Understanding doesn't mean forgiveness—but it changes everything.

### Act 3: The Choice

She finds it: the crystallized remains of the God of Dawn. Massive. Beautiful. Silent. One of the Hollow God's oldest victims, preserved in the core.

Auriel goes silent. Not manipulating. Not afraid. Just... present. Waiting.

Seraphina has a choice:

1. **Purge Auriel.** Cast out the fragment, reclaim her body completely, become purely herself. The radiance will fade, but the violation ends.

2. **Merge with Auriel.** Accept the fragment as part of herself. Not possession—partnership. Become a true Celestial: mortal and divine intertwined. Keep the power, shared equally.

3. **Release Auriel to the Crystal.** Let the fragment rejoin its source. Give up the power, but give Auriel peace. An act of mercy for something that wronged her.

Whatever she chooses, she faces the Hollow God next. And she faces it knowing who she truly is.

**Theme:** Identity is what you choose to keep.

## Personality

Seraphina is **angry** and hides it behind radiance.

For twelve years, she smiled while being used. She comforted others while something ate her faith from the inside. She learned to perform holiness while feeling hollow.

Now that she knows the truth, the anger is surfacing. Sharp comments. Bitter observations. A tendency to push back when others assume she'll be the healer, the comforter, the saint.

But beneath the anger is something older: a desperate desire to be *real*. To be Sara again—or Seraphina, if that's who she's become—but to be *herself*. To have her own thoughts, her own feelings, her own light.

### Voice Examples

**Combat Start:** "Let's see if this light is worth what it cost me."

**Using Radiance:** "I didn't ask for this power. But I'm keeping it."

**Entering Divine Form:** "This isn't Auriel's light. This is *mine*."

**Taking Damage:** "Even saints bleed. Ask me how I know."

**Killing Blow:** "Consider yourself blessed. I'm not feeling merciful."

**Low HP:** "I've survived possession. I'll survive this."

**Victory:** "Still radiant. Still standing. Still *me*."

## Radiance Mechanic: Narrative Meaning

**Radiance** is the divine energy flowing through Seraphina—originally Auriel's, but increasingly her own.

### Building Radiance
Each point of Radiance is Seraphina *claiming* the power that was taken from her. Not asking permission. Not channeling something else. Taking ownership.

*"Blessing"* isn't Seraphina praying—it's her *demanding* what was always hers.

### Divine Form
At 10 Radiance, Seraphina enters Divine Form. This isn't Auriel ascending—it's Sara/Seraphina fully inhabiting the power that was forced on her.

She doesn't become the vessel. She becomes the *source*.

### Consuming Radiance
Using Smite the Wicked consumes all Radiance—a massive expenditure of claimed power. It's not desperate; it's *decisive*. Spending everything you've gathered because this moment matters.
